My approach

I believe in personalized education. Everyone needs to go at a pace that they are comfortable to achieve an efficient learning outcome.
I believe that teachers need to employ a variety of teaching strategies in the classroom; with scaffolding being the anchor to whatever strategy is employed. This includes carefully-designed individual learning activities, group work and whole-class instruction, and connecting learning to the real world. I believe that successful outcomes depend on the quality of learner engagement and the opportunities provided for feedback between teacher and student. I believe that whole class instruction and one and one tutoring can be a highly effective instructional approach if it includes discussion and learners have the opportunity to respond and contribute.
I believe in multiple and varied assessment practices that regularly inform students if they are reaching their objectives. These assessment techniques also allow learners to understand where they are on their learning journey and how they can improve.
My approach is one that relates concepts and skills in such a way that students learn to understand and appreciate the nature of Economics and what constitutes excellence for the developmental stage they are at. I seek to engage with learners’ mental ideas about the really important concepts, via a journey of discovery. This approach requires learners to revisit concepts over an extended period of time and within different contexts. This spiralling strategy reinforces learning and leads to deeper levels of understanding.
Ultimately, my only goal is to have students perform at their optimal level of performance as opposed to a functional level which they could easily achieve on their own. In short my overall approach is geared towards raising student performance while preparing them to be independent, self-regulated learners equipped for modern life.
